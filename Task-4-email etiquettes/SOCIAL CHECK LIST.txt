Social Media. Don'ts for College Students

A checklist for college students to use media in a smart way.

✅ DOs

1. Think before you post. Ask yourself if you are okay with a boss, teacher or parent seeing your post. If not do not post it.

2. Keep your stuff private. Do not share where you live your phone number or what you do every day with everyone.

3. Use passwords for all your accounts. Turn on security to protect your accounts from people who should not access them.

4. When sharing someones stuff give them credit. Mention the person who created it and respect their work.

5. Be mindful of what you leave online. Check who can see your posts. Clean up posts or tagged photos that do not show who you are now.

6. Verify information before sharing it. Check facts using sources. Do not spread information.

7. Be kind in discussions. If you disagree, do politely and avoid personal attacks. Always show respect.

❌ DON'Ts

1. Do not post when you are emotional or upset. It can hurt your relationships. How people see you. Take a moment to think before posting.

2. Do not share information. This includes exam papers, internal college communications or personal details about others.

3. Do not spread hurtful messages online. If you see someone being bullied online do not share it. Report it. Support the person being targeted.

4. Do not accept friend. Follow requests from people you do not know. Be careful about who can see your profile.

5. Do not use media to cheat. Give credit when using someones work. It is not right. Can be, against the law.

6. Do not forget about your health. Limit scrolling and comparing. Unfollow or mute accounts that make you feel bad.
