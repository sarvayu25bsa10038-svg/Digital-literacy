## 8 Actionable Tips to Stay Safe from Cybercrime
*(for College Students in India)*

---

**1. NEVER share your UPI PIN, OTP, or CVV with anyone.**
Your bank, Google Pay, PhonePe, or any app will NEVER ask for your PIN. If someone asks — it is a scam.

---

**2. [FINANCIAL/UPI SAFETY] Always verify before accepting collect requests.**
A "collect request" on UPI means money will be **TAKEN** from you, not sent to you. Never enter your PIN to "receive" money.

---

**3. [FINANCIAL/UPI SAFETY] Download apps only from official sources (Play Store / App Store).**
Fake banking and UPI apps are used to steal credentials. Always check the developer name and ratings before installing.

---

**4. Use strong, unique passwords for every account.**
Avoid using your name, birthdate, or "1234" as passwords. Use a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols. Change passwords every 3–6 months.

---

**5. Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) on all accounts.**
Even if your password is stolen, 2FA ensures no one can log in without access to your phone or email.

---

**6. Do not click on unknown links received via SMS, WhatsApp, or email.**
Phishing links often look like real websites. Always check the URL carefully before entering any information.

---

**7. Be cautious while using public Wi-Fi.**
Avoid logging into bank accounts or making payments on public networks. Use a VPN if necessary.

---

**8. Regularly check your bank statements and UPI transaction history.**
Report any unknown or suspicious transaction immediately to your bank and cybercrime authorities.
