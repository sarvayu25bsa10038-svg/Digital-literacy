PART A – CASE STUDY: UPI / ONLINE PAYMENT FRAUD
----------------------------------------------------------------

What is UPI Payment Fraud?

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is one of the fastest-growing
cybercrimes in India. It involves criminals tricking victims into
transferring money or revealing sensitive payment credentials through
deception, fake links, or social engineering.

How It Typically Happens (Step-by-Step):

Step 1 – The fraudster contacts the victim via phone call, SMS, or
WhatsApp, posing as a bank official, customer care representative,
or even a friend in need.

Step 2 – They create a sense of urgency. For example: "Your account
will be blocked in 2 hours unless you verify your UPI PIN" or "I
need to send you money urgently, please accept this request."

Step 3 – The victim is sent a collect request (a payment request,
not a send request) or a fake link that looks like an official
bank page.

Step 4 – The victim, believing they are receiving money or securing
their account, enters their UPI PIN — which actually authorizes
a payment out of their account.

Step 5 – Money is instantly transferred. UPI transactions are
irreversible, making recovery extremely difficult.

Who is Usually Targeted?

- College students and first-time digital payment users
- Senior citizens unfamiliar with UPI mechanics
- Online sellers on platforms like OLX or Facebook Marketplace
- People seeking part-time jobs or loan assistance online

Consequences:

- Immediate financial loss (cases range from a few hundred to
  several lakhs of rupees)
- Emotional distress, anxiety, and loss of trust in digital systems
- Difficulty in recovering funds as transactions are instant
- In severe cases, victims fall into debt or face legal complications

Realistic Scenario:

Priya, a 19-year-old college student, received a WhatsApp message
from an unknown number claiming to be from her bank. The message
said her UPI account was compromised and she needed to verify it
immediately by entering her PIN on a shared link. Believing it to
be genuine, she clicked the link, entered her PIN, and lost Rs. 8,500
within seconds. By the time she realized what had happened, the money
was gone and the number was switched off.

PART B – STAY SAFE ONLINE: PREVENTION CHECKLIST
----------------------------------------------------------------

8 Actionable Tips to Stay Safe from Cybercrime (for College Students in India)

1. NEVER share your UPI PIN, OTP, or CVV with anyone.
   Your bank, Google Pay, PhonePe, or any app will NEVER ask
   for your PIN. If someone asks — it is a scam.

2. [FINANCIAL/UPI SAFETY] Always verify before accepting
   collect requests.
   A "collect request" on UPI means money will be TAKEN from
   you, not sent to you. Never enter your PIN to "receive" money.

3. [FINANCIAL/UPI SAFETY] Download apps only from official
   sources (Play Store / App Store).
   Fake banking and UPI apps are used to steal credentials.
   Always check the developer name and ratings before installing.

4. Use strong, unique passwords for every account.
   Avoid using your name, birthdate, or "1234" as passwords.
   Use a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols. Change passwords
   every 3–6 months.

5. Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) on all accounts.
   Even if your password is stolen, 2FA ensures no one can log
   in without access to your phone or email.

6. Do not click on unknown links received via SMS, WhatsApp,
   or email.
   Phishing links often look like real websites. Always check
   the URL carefully before entering any information.

7. Be cautious while using public Wi-Fi.
   Avoid logging into bank accounts or making payments on
   public networks. Use a VPN if necessary.

8. Regularly check your bank statements and UPI transaction
   history.
   Report any unknown or suspicious transaction immediately to
   your bank and cybercrime authorities.

REPORTING CHANNELS:
- National Cyber Crime Portal: www.cybercrime.gov.in
- Cybercrime Helpline Number: 1930
- Report UPI fraud directly to your bank's customer care.

ITEM 26 – REFLECTION (100–150 words)
----------------------------------------------------------------

What Surprised Me Most and What I Will Change

While researching UPI payment fraud, what surprised me most was
how simple and convincing these scams actually are. I had always
assumed that only older or less educated people fell for online
fraud. However, I discovered that even educated, tech-savvy college
students are targeted — and many fall victim simply because they
act quickly without thinking.

The most shocking fact was that entering your UPI PIN on a collect
request sends money out of your account, not into it. This small
but critical misunderstanding has caused thousands of people to
lose their savings in seconds.

The habit I will personally change is this: I will pause and verify
before acting on any urgent financial message or call, no matter
how official it looks or sounds. Urgency is the scammer's most
powerful weapon — and patience is mine.
